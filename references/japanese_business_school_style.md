# Japanese Business School Research Plan Style

## Core Premise

For Japanese business/management graduate school applications, a research plan is a compressed thesis design. It shows whether the student has the vision, attitude, literature understanding, method awareness, and feasibility to complete a master's thesis.

## Recommended Structure

1. Research theme
2. Research background
3. Prior research review
4. Problem awareness and research purpose
5. Research method and analysis method
6. Data collection plan
7. Expected results
8. Academic contribution and practical implication
9. References and background materials

## Evaluation Lens

- Can the topic be said in one sentence?
- Is it problem awareness, not only phenomenon description?
- Does the research question come from prior studies?
- Is the research gap justified?
- Are research object, analysis object, and causal relation clear?
- Do theoretical concepts match operational variables?
- Can the method answer the research question?
- Are academic contribution and practical implication separated?
- Does the topic fit the target graduate school or supervisor area?

## Style Rules

- "No prior research exists" is weak by itself.
- "This is trendy" is background, not problem awareness.
- Narrow research object, wider theoretical meaning.
- One sentence, one claim.
- One paragraph, one role.
- Mediators explain why.
- Moderators explain when or for whom.
- If claiming causality, check time order, alternative causes, and operationalization.

## Common Problems

- Treating interest in a phenomenon as problem awareness
- Overly large topic
- Literature review as paper-by-paper summary
- Variable stacking
- Cross-level theory misuse
- Method name-dropping
- Practical implication without academic contribution
- Using websites, news, or market reports as prior research instead of background sources
