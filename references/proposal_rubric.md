# Proposal Rubric

Use this to diagnose a full or partial research proposal.

## Evaluation Dimensions

### 1. Topic And Scope

Strong: Research object, context, population, outcome, and boundary are clear.

Weak: Topic is broad, trendy, or descriptive.

Red flags: "XX importance," "current situation of XX," "strategy of XX" without a researchable relation.

### 2. Research Question Or Hypothesis

Strong: Specific, answerable, and derived from prior research.

Weak: Interesting topic, but not testable or too broad.

Check: Is it asking whether, why, when, how, for whom, or which path matters more?

### 3. Literature Review And Synthesis

Strong: Groups papers by concepts, theories, methods, or debates and shows relationships.

Weak: Summarizes paper by paper.

Check: Does the review naturally lead to the student's research question?

### 4. Research Gap

Strong: Precise, consequential, and supported by literature.

Weak: "Few studies" or "not enough research" without proof.

Check: What exactly remains unexplained?

### 5. Theory And Conceptual Logic

Strong: Key constructs are defined, and theory explains why variables connect.

Weak: Theory is named but not used.

Check: Does the theory explain X -> M, M -> Y, X -> Y, or a boundary condition?

### 6. Method Fit

Strong: Method, sample, measurement, and analysis match the research question.

Weak: Method names are listed without design logic.

Red flags: Strong causality from one-time survey; SEM/ANOVA used as decoration.

### 7. Feasibility

Strong: Scope, access, sample, timeline, and resources are realistic.

Weak: Depends on inaccessible data or an oversized design.

### 8. Contribution

Strong: Contribution maps to the gap: mechanism, boundary, context, method, measurement, or practical insight.

Weak: "Enrich research" or "provide reference" without a target.

## Output Template

- Overall diagnosis
- Strongest part
- Weakest logic
- Gap quality
- Variable/model quality
- Method fit
- Feasibility
- Next revision task
